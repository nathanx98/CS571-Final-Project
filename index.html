Introduction

According to the City of Boston Website,

"311 is an easy-to-remember telephone number that connects you with highly-trained Constituent Service Center representatives who are ready to help you with requests for non-emergency City services and information."

The line is 24/7, and can also be reached with a mobile application. Boston City Government releases a dataset containing every 311 call during each calendar year. This page is based off the data from 2020, the most recent full year dataset available. This dataset includes information for each case such as time, location, category, completed, and ontime completion. It provides a view of the city created by its citizens and our visualization hopes to provide a small town view of the happenings of New England's largest city.

311 Calls by City Council District

city council district

City Council members have the power to approve the budget, to make decisions over city agencies, and to approve legislative proposals. Their purpose is to represent the wishes of the citizens of their district. Therefore, they are those citizens' point of contact for having their voice heard in city decisions. Without knowing what their citizens think about city government, they cannot do their jobs. This visualization shows the most common types of 311 cases in each Boston City Council district, and how many were completed on time.

The first chart shows the total amount of cases completed on time and the total amount of those that weren't. The second graph shows the top 10 most common types of 311 cases in a district, split into cases completed on time and cases that weren't. The third chart shows the top 10 most commonly overdue cases, split in the same fashion.

Overdue Cases by Type

Cases that were completed in an untimely fashion are reported by this dataset, and it is an interesting category to look into. Below are the most commonly overdue types of cases across the whole city, with the circles' area representing their size. Does the city consider these kinds of cases to be a lower priority? Are there simply too many to handle? Is the city 311 department understaffed? The answers to those questions we can't know from the dataset, but there is something to gain from seeing what isn't being taken care of in Boston.

Cases Per Month

There was an exact moment in 2020 when everything changed. The dip in cases in April is the most immediate feature of this graph. Besides that fall in cases showing the start of US lockdown, one can gain an insight of the seasonal needs of the city. Unshoveled sidewalks is a very common case during the cold New England winter months, and improper trash storage gets on people's nerves more during the hot summer months.

Quiet and Clean Neighborhoods

The 311 data is a portrait of the city through a wide lens, something not only useful to current citizens, but to incoming residents as well. Everybody wants to live in a clean, quiet neighborhood, and the various noise complaints and sanitary complaints in this dataset can show where that place is (or isn't). This interactive map shows points representing cases of unsavory conditions. Scrolling the mouse wheel will zoom, and clicking and dragging will move the map.
